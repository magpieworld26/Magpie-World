⚓ Scene 2 — The Captain's Quarters · Aboard the Windbreaker

The compass is slick with blood. Outside, cannon fire rattles the hull of the Windbreaker, but the real threat is inside — standing in the ruined doorway with a blade still dripping red.

Quartermaster Silas fills the frame. He's a broad man, built like the ship's keel, and he moves with the unhurried confidence of someone who has already decided how this ends. His cutlass catches the amber lantern light as it sweeps toward you in a slow, deliberate arc — a warning, not yet a strike.

His cold eyes lock onto your closed fist.

'The Captain was a fool to trust a greenhorn,' Silas growls over the din of battle, his voice low enough that only you can hear it above the thunder of cannon and splintering wood. 'Give me the compass, and I might just let you take a lifeboat.'

The brass in your hand pulses with a strange, unnatural warmth. It doesn't point north. The needle is spinning wildly — then stops. It locks, with eerie certainty, directly onto Silas.

The compass vibrates faintly against your palm — almost as if it recognises something in Silas, something carried close to his chest beneath that salt-stained coat. Whatever secret this instrument holds, it chose you to keep it.
